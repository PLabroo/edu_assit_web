RESOURCES

Textbooks and sample papers have long been the most coveted study material for students. Anyone who has performed well in a competitive or board exam of any sort always hails these as the ultimate prep tool. What about them is it that makes them the best resource for students? For the learners the textbook is one of the most important sources of contact they have with the language. It is a framework or guide that helps them to organize their learning. It is helpful to involve students in the process of adapting textbooks. The sample paper is based on the actual question paper which helps students in getting an overview of the paper pattern including the type of questions like short or long answer type questions, mostly asked topics, marks etc. With sample papers, students can practice better to prepare for the exam and consequently, they are relieved from exam pressure and nervousness.

GATE

Links

Internalize the GATE syllabus: Before diving in, you need to know what is in the pool. So, go through the official syllabus of GATE 2019. It will give you a clear idea regarding the topics you need to study for the entrance exam.

Check the GATE 2019 Exam Pattern:The exam pattern of GATE 2019 will give candidates hints on how they should prepare for the exam.

Set in place a study plan:Once you know what is in the syllabus and know the exam pattern, next step is to chalk up a study plan.

GRE

Links

Memorize Direction: You can save time and cut down on test-day surprises by learning the format and directions of the test inside and out. Knowing the test format is an essential part of any GRE study plan.

Easy questions first:Within a subsection, all GRE questions are worth the same amount of points. This means it makes sense to collect the easy points first!

Read Carefully:Unless you’re doing some rapid guessing to close out a section, it’s critical that you read questions closely.
