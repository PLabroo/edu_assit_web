MASTER OF BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION

What is Master Of Business Administration?

Master of Business Administration or MBA is one of the most popular post-graduate programmes in India and abroad. The two-year programme is a gateway to a plethora of job opportunities in the corporate world, primarily at the managerial level. What makes MBA such a popular course is that students from all the streams, including Science, Commerce and Humanities can pursue it.A regular MBA or Post-Graduation Diploma in Management (PGDM) is generally a two-year course divided into four-six semesters. However, there are certain private institutes that also offer one-year PGDM programmes. One can pursue MBA in various modes, including regular, online and distance education. Interested candidates can also find programmes customised based on their professional experience such as Executive MBA.

EXAMS

CAT

Common Admission Test

The Common Admission Test (CAT) is a computer based test for admission in a graduate management program. The test scores a candidate on the bases of Quantitative Ability (QA), Verbal Ability (VA) and Reading Comprehension (RC), Data Interpretation (DI) and Logical Reasoning (LR). The Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) started this exam and use the test for selecting students for their business administration programs. The test is conducted every year by one of the IIMs based on a policy of rotation.

XAT

Xavier Admission Test

Xavier Aptitude Test or popularly known as XAT is a national-level management entrance test conducted by XLRI, Jamshedpur, for admission to management programs at XLRI and other XAT participating institutes across the country. XAT 2020 will be conducted on January 5, 2020, in online (computer-based) mode. Around 75,000 candidates are expected to appear for XAT 2020.The overall difficulty level of XAT is high.

GMAT

Graduate Management Admission Test

The Graduate Management Admission Test (GMAT) is a computer adaptive test (CAT) intended to assess certain analytical, writing, quantitative, verbal, and reading skills in written English for use in admission to a graduate management program, such as an MBA program.It requires knowledge of certain specific grammar and knowledge of certain specific algebra, geometry, and arithmetic.It is conducted by Graduate Management Admissions Council.

Registrations for CAT 2019 have been the highest in the last ten years, with 2.44 lakhs candidates applying this year. CAT is the gateway to 20 IIMs and other top B-schools. Eligibility

  1. Nationality

    • Candidates with Indian citizenship are eligible to apply for CAT.

  2. Educational Qualification

    Any graduate with 50% marks is eligible to take CAT 2019 exam. Talking about the eligibility criteria for CAT 2019, a candidate must have any of the following:

    1. Bachelor's Degree with at least 50% marks or equivalent CGPA (45% marks for SC/ST/PWD categories) or
    2. An equivalent examination recognized by the MHRD, Government of India; including AMICE of the Institution of Civil Engineers (India)
    3. Candidates appearing in the final year of Bachelor’s degree or waiting for their result are also eligible.

  3. Age Criteria

    There is no age limit(minimum & maximum) prescribed for the candidates who are applying for CAT examination.

  4. Number of attempts

    There is an 8-attempt overall lifetime limit.

  5. Essential Pointers for Reserved Candidates
    1. The applicant must acquire at least 45% marks or equivalent CGPA for a Bachelor's Degree or equivalent if the candidate falls under the categories of Scheduled Tribe (ST), Scheduled Caste (SC) or Person with Disability (PwD).
    2. Amongst the total seats available each year for contesting, a sum of 10% of the seats are reserved for Economically Weaker Sections (EWS) applicants. Scheduled Caste (SC) applicants see a quota of 15% of the seats reserved for themselves, while Scheduled Tribe (ST) and Other Backward Classes from the 'non-creamy' layer (NC-OBC) have 7.5% and 27% of the seats reserved respectively.
    3. Applicants with Benchmark Disability meaning people with not less than 40% of an identified disability certified by an authoritative body have 5% of the seats reserved as per the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act sanctioned in 2016.

CAT Selection Process

CAT selection process was updated in 2015 for newer IIMs. 10 out of 20 IIMs still adhere to the traditional selection process i.e. GD, WTA, and PI rounds. While 10 IIMs are opting for the new process, this year. The new process also called Common Admission Process (CAP) will be conducted by IIM Ranchi. IIM Kozhikode is responsible for conducting CAT throughout the country.

CAT Selection Process: Written Test, GD or Written Ability Test, and Personal Interview

  1. Written Test

    2. The written test is divided into three main sections, with a total of 100 questions to be attempted in a duration of 3 hours.

    Section No. of the Questions Total Marks Duration
    Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VARC) 34 102 60 minutes
    Quantitative Ability (QA) 34 102 60 minutes
    Total 100 300 180

    The average cut off for most of the IIMs would range from 95-99%. To know more about calculating percentile score and the section-wise cut-off, please click here.

  2. GD or Written Ability Test

    3. Only those candidates will make it to the second round who have cleared the cut-off of the written test. IIMs and other top B-Schools will be calling the applicants, inviting them for the next step.

    • The next step of the process varies across the institute. Some of the institutes like IIM Ahmedabad, IIM Lucknow, etc. will conduct both GD and WAT, along with PI.
    • While some of the other institutes will directly conduct the Written Test.
    • For Group discussion (GD), candidates are divided into a group of 8-10 each. The purpose of GD is to check your thought process and teamwork abilities.
    • For written ability test (WAT) you will be given 15-45 minutes depending upon the respective IIM.

    The general purpose of each process is tabulated below:

    Stage Purpose
    Group Discussion To check if you are a team player
    Written Ability Test To check how clearly you can convey your message.
    Personal Interview To check your confidence, background, and other details.
  3. Personal Interview

    4. The Personal Interview round or simply PI round holds a weightage of around 30-40% of the entire process. Candidates usually face a panel of 2-4 interviewers.

    While preparing a final cut-off for the admissions, IIMs considers the following factors:

    • Overall CAT score
    • Academic Qualification (from Xth standard onwards)
    • Overall work experience
    • Extra-curricular activities
    • Academic Diversity (Preference to non-B.tech graduates over B. Tech graduates)
    • Gender Diversity (Preference to girls over boys)

    Note:-The weightage for each of these factors varies with the institute.
